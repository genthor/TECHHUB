Why Assistive Tech Matters

Screen Readers

Software that reads text aloud for people with visual impairments. It turns digital information into spoken words, making websites, documents, and apps accessible.

Voice Control

Speech-to-text and voice command systems allow people with mobility challenges to navigate devices, write messages, and control their environment using only their voice.

Mobility Aids

From smart wheelchairs to adaptive controllers, mobility technology helps people with physical disabilities move freely and interact with the world around them.

Captioning & Transcription

Real-time captioning and transcription tools make audio content accessible for people with hearing impairments, ensuring no one misses out on important information.

Dyslexia-Friendly Tools

Special fonts, color overlays, and reading aids help people with dyslexia process text more easily, making reading less frustrating and more enjoyable.

AI-Powered Assistants

Smart assistants like Alexa, Siri, and Google Assistant help people with various disabilities perform daily tasks—from setting reminders to controlling smart home devices.

Why We Advocate

Many People Are Left Behind

Millions of people with disabilities lack access to basic assistive technology because of cost, lack of awareness, or limited availability.

Technology Changes Lives

With the right tools, people with disabilities can study, work, and participate fully in society. Assistive tech is not a luxury—it's a necessity.

We All Benefit

Inclusive design and assistive technology benefit everyone—not just people with disabilities. Accessible products are better products for all.
